Julia's Vintage Clothing

About Us

Drenched in the vibrant hues of the hip '70s, Julia's Vintage Clothing is a family legacy that blossomed from the creative minds of Olivia and James Wattson. In the heart of London's Leadenhall Market, they birthed the iconic J&O Flowers, a haven for bespoke tailoring and a treasure trove of haberdashery delights. For five decades, the Wattsons stitched stories into every garment until they gracefully closed the chapter of J&O Flowers and embraced the well-deserved embrace of retirement.

Fast forward to the late 2020s, where the torch of this illustrious family tradition was passed to none other than Julia, their spirited great-granddaughter. With a nod to the past, Julia courageously resurrected the family business, dusting off J&O's old patented designs and unveiling a trove of vintage clothing hidden in the attic. In a flamboyant rebranding, Julia's Vintage Clothing now proudly carries the legacy forward, aiming to weave the same family values and creative spirit into the fabric of Greenwich. The journey continues, and Julia's is here to color the world once more with the timeless charm of '70s fashion and a commitment to the warmth that family brings.

With our team of tailors and designers, we take old unusable materials and clothing and breathe new life into the fabrics. Keeping to the groovy flower power that sparked life into the company, we design disco fever denim, peace and love leggings and vintage outfits that remind us of a more freeing era of fashion.

Offering upcycling classes, workshops on sewing and repairing your old treads, and hosting clothing drives with our partner charities in the area, there's never a dull week in Julia's.
